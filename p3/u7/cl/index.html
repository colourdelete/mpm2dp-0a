Cosine Law

The cosine law is as follows: $sa^2=b^2+c^2-2bc(cosA)$e .

The cosine law can be used to find a side length from the two other sides and the angle between them.

The proof is as follows: Assume a triangle $s\triangle ABC$e , where, $sh$e is a line from $sB$e to $sD$e (which is a point on $s\overline{AC}$e ) where, $s\angle BDC=\angle BDA=90^{\circ}$e .

From the cosine ratio: $scosC=\frac{CD}{a}$e , or $sCS=acosC$e

$sDA=b-acosC$e $sBD=asinC$e

Using the pythegorean theorem, $sc^2=(BD)^2+(DA)^2$e , or $sc^2=(asinC)^2+(b-acosC)^2$e , or $sc^2=a^2+b^2-2abcosC$e .
