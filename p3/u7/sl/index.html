Sine Law

The sine law is as follows: $s\frac{a}{\sin{A}} \,=\, \frac{b}{\sin{B}} \,=\, \frac{c}{\sin{C}} \,=\, 2R$e , where $sa$e , $sb$e , and $sc$e , are the lengths of the sides of the triangle, and $s\alpha$e , $s\beta$e , and $s\gamma$e , are the opposite angles of the sides, and $sR$e is the radius of the triangle’s circumcircle.

The sine law can be used to find the other sides of an acute triangle when two angles and a side are given.

The proof is as follows:

Assume a triangle $s\triangle ABC$e , where, $sh$e is a line from $sA$e to $sD$e (which is a point on $s\overline{BC}$e ) where, $s\angle ADC=\angle ADB=90^{\circ}$e .

Using the definiton of the sine function, $ssinB=\frac{h}{c}$e , and $ssinC=\frac{h}{b}$e .

They can be rewritten as $sh=csinB$e , and $sh=bsinC$e , or $scsinB=bsinC$e .

Therefore, $s\frac{c}{sinC}=\frac{b}{sinB}$e .

Repeating the above on line $sj$e , which goes from $sb$e to $sE$e (whcih is a point on $s\overline{AC}$e ) where, $s\angle BEA=\angle BEC=90^{\circ}$e , yields $s\frac{c}{sinC}=\frac{a}{sinA}$e .
