Sine and Cosine Ratio

Similar to tangents, sines and cosines can be defined as ratios as well.

$$ssin\theta=\frac{opposite}{hypotenuse}$$e $$scos\theta=\frac{adjacent}{hypotenuse}$$e $$stan\theta=\frac{opposite}{adjacent}$$e

A easy way to remember this is using “soh-cah-toa”. “soh” meaning “sin = opposite / hypotenuse”, and so on.

Remember that you can only use these when they involve right triangles.

You can use these to find:

  • an angle from two side lengths, or
  • one side length from another side length and one acute angle
Example Determine the three primary trig ratios for $s\angle C$e , where $s\overline{AB}=12$e , $s\overline{BC}=15$e , and $s\overline{CA}=9$e , for $s\triangle ABC$e . $$ssinC=\frac{12}{15}$$e $$scosC=\frac{9}{15}$$e $$stanC=\frac{12}{9}$$e
