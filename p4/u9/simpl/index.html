Simplfication of Polynomials

Simplifications of polynomials involve combining like terms.

Example $s\frac{3x+2}{3y-1}$e where $sx=-2$e , and $sy=1$e is: $$s\frac{3x+2}{3y-1}$$e $$s=\frac{4}{2}$$e $$s=2$$e
Example The non-permissible value(s) for $s\frac{x^3+1}{y-3}$e is: If $sy-3$e is $s0$e , the answer would be non-permissible. $$sy=3$$e
Example The non-permissible value(s) for $$s\frac{2a^2+4a-48}{3a^2+3a-60}$$e $$s=\frac{3(a+6)}{2(a+5)}$$e is: $$sa\ne -5,4$$e
