Domain and Range

For any function $g(x)$, get the parent function $f(x)$ and put it into the following form: $$g(x)=af(k(x-d))+c$$

Each parent function has the domain and range (common ones are listed in p4/u8/pfn#common-parent-functions ). To get the domain and range of the function, substitute the $x$ in the domain and the $y$ in the range to $x-d$ and $y-c$.

Example To get the domain and range of $(x)$, where $f(x)=\sqrt{x}$, and where $g(x)=\sqrt{x-3}=af(k(x-d))+c$, substitute $x-d$ for $x$ and $y-c$ for $y$: $d=3, d\in \mathbb{R}, x-d=x-3$
$c=0, y-c=y, c\in \mathbb{R}$
$D\{x+d\in \mathbb{R} | x-d\ge 0\}$
$D\{x\in \mathbb{R} | x\ge 3\}$
$R\{y+c\in \mathbb{R} | y-c\ge 0\}$
$R\{y\in \mathbb{R} | y\ge 0\}$
