Negative and Zero Exponents

Negative exponents turn the number into its reciprocal, and an exponent of $s0$e is always $s1$e .

$$sx^0=1$$e $$sx^-1=\frac{1}{x}$$e

Example $$s(\frac{2}{3})^{-2}=(\frac{3}{2})^2=\frac{9}{4}$$e
